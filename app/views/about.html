Bob Hansel

Phone: (724) 858-0288
Email: hanselbob@yahoo.com

Bob resides in Ligonier, Pennsylvania. Songwriter musician, poet and humorist. Bob has been playing music professionally for over 25 years, formerly as bassist and vocalist for Pittsburgh’s Back Up & Push plus The Bo Sloan Band, which was voted the Number #1 Band in Pittsburgh in 1982, The Knobs (Irwin), Nighthawk (1) and Solo from 1998 to 2003. Bob’s recording credits include bassist on the first album released by Back Up & Push, Bo Sloan’s “High Livin’” album, and as a session artist for many other Pittsburgh based groups.

Bob released his first solo effort entitled “The Dream”, locally December 23rd, 1993. “Garrett County” CD Released August 2003. Bob is currently recording his 3rd CD in his recording studio, soon to be released.

Bob plays; acoustic guitar, bass guitar, mandolin, harmonica, lead and harmony vocals plus an avid songwriter. Past bands I have had the pleasure of working with: Atlantis, Blue Spruce, Main Street, Back Up & Push, The Bo Sloan Band, The Knobs, Nighthawk, and as soloist

Steve Budesky

Phone: (724) 672-5084

“Stevie B.” Hails from Greensburg, Pennsylvania. He started playing guitar his senior year in high school. He had played on 2 hit records by the “Vogues”, and had backed up over 100 hit vocal bands. In 1966 Steve moved to New York City where he landed the conductor/guitarist spot for “Neil Diamond” for 6 years playing on Solitary Man, Cracklin Rosie, and Sweet Caroline. Steve stayed on with Neil till he moved to L.A. in 1972. He then recorded and backed up Cher, Diana Ross, and The Spinners. Steve also played on “I’ll Be Around”. He has played for country artists, Freddy Fender, Stella Parton, Loretta Lynn, Juice Newton and many others. Steve was on the road playing music for over 25 years. His only local Pittsburgh bands were “Lois Scott” and Back up & Push plus the “Rick Parker Band” playing at the Royce Hotel, Pittsburgh Airport for 5 years 6 nights a week as the house band.

Steve enjoys the intimacy of the duo with Bob challenging them to sing and play their very best!
